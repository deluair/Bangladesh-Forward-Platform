Our Policy Platform

Economic Development

The Challenge:

Despite steady GDP growth, income inequality continues to widen. The economy remains vulnerable to external shocks, and job creation has not kept pace with the growing workforce, especially for educated youth.

Our Solution:

Implement a diversified economic strategy that focuses on high-value industries beyond garments, including IT, pharmaceuticals, and sustainable agriculture. Create special economic zones with tax incentives for startups and SMEs. Launch a national skills development program aligned with industry needs to prepare youth for the jobs of tomorrow.

Climate Resilience

The Challenge:

Bangladesh remains one of the world's most climate-vulnerable nations, facing rising sea levels, increasing cyclone intensity, and unpredictable rainfall patterns. These threats endanger lives, infrastructure, agriculture, and economic stability.

Our Solution:

Develop a comprehensive climate adaptation plan including elevated infrastructure, expanded mangrove forests as natural buffers, and climate-resilient agriculture. Increase renewable energy to 40% of power generation by 2035. Implement a carbon tax on largest emitters to fund climate adaptation projects. Lead global advocacy for climate justice and finance for vulnerable nations.

Governance Reform

The Challenge:

Corruption, bureaucratic inefficiency, and weakened democratic institutions have eroded public trust and hindered national development. Citizens face obstacles in accessing government services and having their voices heard.

Our Solution:

Establish an independent anti-corruption commission with prosecutorial powers. Implement e-governance systems for all public services to increase transparency and reduce corruption. Reform civil service through merit-based recruitment and promotion. Strengthen local government with devolution of power and resources. Ensure independence of judiciary and election commission.

Education Revolution

The Challenge:

Bangladesh's education system struggles with outdated curricula, inadequate facilities, and teaching methods that emphasize memorization over critical thinking. Many students graduate without skills needed for the modern job market.

Our Solution:

Revamp national curriculum to focus on critical thinking, problem-solving, and digital literacy. Invest 4% of GDP in education with emphasis on teacher training and classroom technology. Establish vocational training centers in every district with industry partnerships. Create research grants for universities and incentivize innovation. Implement universal early childhood education.

Healthcare for All

The Challenge:

Healthcare services remain inaccessible to many, especially in rural areas. Out-of-pocket expenses drive families into poverty, and the system struggles with shortages of qualified healthcare workers, medication, and modern equipment.

Our Solution:

Implement a universal health coverage system with a national health insurance program. Establish fully staffed community clinics in every rural ward. Expand digital health services to connect remote areas with specialists. Create incentives for doctors to serve in rural areas. Invest in domestic pharmaceutical manufacturing to ensure affordable medication.

Infrastructure Transformation

The Challenge:

Inadequate transportation, energy, and digital infrastructure constrains economic growth and quality of life. Traffic congestion, power shortages, and limited internet connectivity hamper productivity and investment.

Our Solution:

Develop integrated multi-modal transportation systems connecting all districts. Expand renewable energy capacity and modernize the power grid. Build climate-resilient coastal infrastructure. Ensure nationwide 5G connectivity and establish digital hubs in every division. Implement smart city technologies in urban centers to improve efficiency and reduce environmental impact.

Employment Generation

The Challenge:

Bangladesh faces rising graduate unemployment, with nearly 40% of university graduates struggling to find suitable jobs. The mismatch between education and industry needs creates frustration and economic waste.

Our Solution:

Launch a National Employment Strategy with industry-specific growth targets. Provide tax incentives for companies hiring first-time job seekers. Establish entrepreneurship hubs in every district with microloan programs. Create digital platforms connecting job seekers with opportunities. Implement work-study programs at universities to provide real-world experience during education.

Disaster Resilience

The Challenge:

Bangladesh's geographic location makes it highly vulnerable to natural disasters including cyclones, floods, and riverbank erosion, causing loss of life, displacement, and economic damage annually.

Our Solution:

Build a comprehensive disaster management system with advanced early warning technology reaching every village. Construct disaster-resistant community shelters in all vulnerable areas. Create a dedicated Disaster Response Corps with specialized training and equipment. Implement community-based disaster preparedness programs. Establish a permanent Climate Adaptation Fund for immediate post-disaster reconstruction.

Urban Transformation

The Challenge:

Rapid urbanization has led to unplanned growth, inadequate housing, traffic congestion, air pollution, and strained public services in Bangladesh's cities, dramatically reducing quality of life.

Our Solution:

Implement comprehensive urban planning with strict zoning laws and green space requirements. Develop affordable housing projects through public-private partnerships. Invest in mass transit systems including expanded metro networks and water transportation. Enact and enforce air quality standards with emissions testing. Create satellite towns to decentralize urban growth and reduce pressure on major cities.

Financial Inclusion

The Challenge:

Despite microfinance success, many Bangladeshis remain unbanked or underbanked. Small businesses struggle to access affordable credit, limiting growth and job creation. The financial system lacks depth and sophistication.

Our Solution:

Expand digital banking services to reach every citizen through mobile technology. Create a tiered regulatory framework to encourage fintech innovation while ensuring consumer protection. Establish specialized SME financing institutions with simplified loan processes. Develop the bond market to provide alternative financing sources. Implement financial literacy programs in schools and communities to increase economic empowerment.

Trade Expansion

The Challenge:

Bangladesh's exports remain concentrated in garments, creating economic vulnerability. Trade infrastructure is underdeveloped, and our products face non-tariff barriers in many markets. Post-LDC graduation will bring new challenges.

Our Solution:

Diversify exports through targeted incentives for high-potential sectors including pharmaceuticals, IT services, and processed foods. Modernize ports and establish efficient customs processes through digitalization. Negotiate strategic free trade agreements focusing on emerging Asian markets. Create export promotion zones with specialized facilities for new export industries. Develop a comprehensive strategy for navigating post-LDC challenges.

Gender Equality

The Challenge:

Despite progress, women in Bangladesh continue to face gender-based violence, workplace discrimination, unequal pay, and barriers to leadership positions in both public and private sectors.

Our Solution:

Establish specialized courts for gender-based violence cases with trained personnel. Create incentives for businesses that promote women to leadership positions. Implement universal childcare programs to support working mothers. Launch girls' education initiative with stipends and mentorship. Ensure equal inheritance rights and economic opportunities through legislative reform and enforcement.
