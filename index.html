Addressing Bangladesh's Critical Challenges

Our nation faces significant challenges that require bold, innovative solutions. Bangladesh Forward is committed to addressing these issues with transparency, integrity, and a people-first approach.

Economic Development

The Challenge:

Despite steady GDP growth, income inequality continues to widen. The economy remains vulnerable to external shocks, and job creation has not kept pace with the growing workforce, especially for educated youth.

Our Solution:

Implement a diversified economic strategy that focuses on high-value industries beyond garments, including IT, pharmaceuticals, and sustainable agriculture. Create special economic zones with tax incentives for startups and SMEs. Launch a national skills development program aligned with industry needs to prepare youth for the jobs of tomorrow.

Climate Resilience

The Challenge:

Bangladesh is one of the most climate-vulnerable countries globally, facing rising sea levels, increased flooding, cyclones, and saltwater intrusion that threatens agriculture and livelihoods of millions.

Our Solution:

Develop a comprehensive Climate Adaptation Fund to finance climate-resilient infrastructure. Implement a nationwide mangrove restoration program to protect coastal areas. Transition to renewable energy with a target of 50% clean energy by 2040. Create climate-smart agricultural practices and provide subsidies for farmers adopting these methods.

Governance Reform

The Challenge:

Systemic corruption, weak institutional capacity, and political polarization have undermined public trust in government and hindered effective service delivery to citizens.

Our Solution:

Establish an independent Anti-Corruption Commission with prosecutorial powers and protection for whistleblowers. Implement e-governance systems for all public services to reduce corruption and increase transparency. Reform the civil service with merit-based recruitment and performance-based promotions. Strengthen local governance through decentralization and participatory budgeting.

Education Revolution

The Challenge:

The education system emphasizes rote learning over critical thinking and fails to prepare students for the modern job market. Quality disparities between urban and rural areas persist, and dropout rates remain high.

Our Solution:

Modernize the curriculum to emphasize critical thinking, problem-solving, and digital literacy. Invest in teacher training and competitive salaries to attract top talent to the profession. Establish a network of technical and vocational schools in partnership with industry. Implement a national digital education platform to bridge the urban-rural divide.

Healthcare for All

The Challenge:

Healthcare remains inaccessible and unaffordable for many citizens, with inadequate facilities in rural areas. Out-of-pocket expenses push millions into poverty annually, and preventable diseases continue to burden the system.

Our Solution:

Implement a Universal Health Coverage system with a focus on preventive care. Establish a network of well-equipped community health centers in every upazila. Launch a national health insurance scheme with subsidies for low-income families. Develop a robust telemedicine infrastructure to connect remote areas with specialized medical expertise.

Infrastructure Transformation

The Challenge:

Inadequate transportation, energy, and digital infrastructure hampers economic growth and quality of life. Urban areas face congestion, pollution, and housing shortages, while rural areas lack basic connectivity.

Our Solution:

Develop an integrated multi-modal transportation system connecting all districts. Implement smart city solutions for urban areas with efficient public transportation and affordable housing. Expand renewable energy capacity and modernize the national grid. Achieve 100% high-speed internet coverage nationwide through public-private partnerships.

Water Security

The Challenge:

Arsenic contamination, groundwater depletion, and pollution of rivers threaten water security. Many communities lack access to clean drinking water, and transboundary water sharing remains contentious.

Our Solution:

Implement a comprehensive river restoration program to clean major waterways. Develop rainwater harvesting and water conservation technologies. Establish a national water quality monitoring system with public access to data. Strengthen diplomatic efforts for equitable water-sharing agreements with neighboring countries.

Youth Empowerment

The Challenge:

With over 65% of the population under 35, Bangladesh has a demographic dividend that remains largely untapped due to unemployment, skills mismatches, and limited opportunities for meaningful civic engagement.

Our Solution:

Create a National Youth Innovation Fund to finance youth-led startups and social enterprises. Establish youth leadership academies in every division to nurture future leaders. Implement a mandatory youth quota in local government committees. Launch a digital platform connecting youth with mentorship, internship, and job opportunities across sectors.

Gender Equality

The Challenge:

Despite progress in girls' education and women's workforce participation, gender-based violence, discrimination, and limited economic and political opportunities for women persist across society.

Our Solution:

Strengthen legal protections against gender-based violence with specialized courts for swift justice. Implement gender-responsive budgeting across all ministries. Provide tax incentives for businesses with gender-equal hiring and promotion practices. Establish women's leadership programs and mentorship networks. Create a comprehensive childcare system to support working mothers.
